What fascinates me

Jay & Veeru Version 2.0

I really love animals. Since I was little, I have had this place in my heart reserved only for animals.I have had many animals as pets . I used to have fish as pets when I was little but because fish required extra care and attention, they weren’t able to last long. I also remember having birds as pets. I remember that they were all budgerigars except for a few which were finches. I used to love them dearly but they also slowly went away. Some escaped whereas some went to heaven .I have also had caterpillars and weird insects as pets too. But the animals that fascinate me the most are turtles and tortoises. They are cute little creatures which only care about food. They don’t have to worry about attending classes or doing their homework all they do is eat, swim and sleep. So I asked my parents if they could get me turtles as pets. I knew it would be almost impossible as certain species of turtles are near extinction and they wouldn’t allow to keep turtles as pets. But, there is this certain species of turtles which originally lived in Mexico but was now exported world wide. I did a ton of research and found out that keeping Red Eared sliders as pets was completely legal. I told this to my parents and they brought me a pair. I named them “Jay” and “Veeru”.(The names are completely inspired by a Bollywood movie so please don’t issue a copyright on me). I still have Jay and Veeru and they both are now three years old. At first they liked eating the market pellets that were available everywhere But, because of the lockdown, these pellets were not available so we had to try feeding them raw meat which they would eat in the wild. And now they only eat meat. The market pellets are completely ignored by them and if we don’t feed them meat, they start getting furious. There have been certain occasions when they got sick. One was during the winters of the year 2020 when I woke up to find Jay’s eye completely swollen. I watched more than a 100 videos on youtube and came to the conclusion that it was because of the deficiency of Vitamin A.That immediate moment I realized it, I started crushing carrots with my bare hands to make a thin mixture of carrot juice and water. I soaked both of them in it every single day and after a few days, his eyes were open and his swelling was gone (“his” because I am never not to confirm their gender. Probably female ) I even made them a huge diy enclosure on our roof. I made it myself using bricks and cement. I don’t know why no one believes it but I worked really hard on it and I have the PROOFS. I have always wanted to make stuff that helps humanity. Even when I was little, I used to make drawings of trucks that could automatically pick up garbage while recycling it and drones that could easily put out a fire within seconds.
